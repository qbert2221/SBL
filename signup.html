№ 02Common Questions

Things you're probably wondering.

Is this financial advice?

No. This is educational content explaining a strategy used by sophisticated investors. Before implementing anything, you should consult a licensed financial advisor, tax professional, and attorney familiar with your specific situation and jurisdiction.

How is this different from a HELOC or margin loan?

HELOCs use home equity as collateral; margin loans typically fund stock trading. The Corporate Dividend Mortgage™ inverts the pattern — the stock funds the home, inside a corporate wrapper that creates structural and tax advantages neither traditional product offers.

How much capital do I need to start?

The classic case study uses $200,000 total ($100K down payment shares + $100K additional capital). The strategy scales down — it works at $50K, just for smaller properties. The calculator on the site lets you test your specific situation.

What if margin rates rise?

This is the single most important risk. The strategy depends on dividend yield exceeding margin cost. If margin rates climb above your dividend yield, the leverage works against you. The playbook covers stress-testing for rate shocks in detail.

What happens if I want to sell the house?

Because the corporation owns everything, you have an additional exit route most homeowners don't: you can sell the corporation rather than just the house. The buyer acquires both the home and any retained portfolio. This typically creates better tax treatment and can fetch a premium. The playbook walks through both exit modes.
